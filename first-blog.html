17 December 2016

Ev3 training held at Hawassa University

This tutoring coincides with the year I started programming.

I know I can't enumerate this as a must-do for beginners in programming but, I can say for me back then it helped me split up compound algorithms into smaller chunks.

The purpose of the tuition was to teach students like myself, how to understand algorithms and build simple but specific programs that the robot understands.

Even though programming the brick wasn't hard, I remember struggling to balance the speed with accuracy while racing our opponents. The race track was made simpy with a black tape, glued to the floor. Our mission was to use the color sensor to detect where the path is and move the motors as fast and as accurate as possible and finish the race.

Another version of the race track was made with card boxe - walls. Now the color sensor is kind of irrelevant. What we used instead was ultrasonic sensor to measure the distance infront of the robot and programmed it to move to certain directions and follow the path to the end line.
